# Campaign Example — The Curse of Mirewatch

A short campaign for **1–4+ heroes**, designed for levels 1–4. Good for a first group.

---

## Overview

The village of Mirewatch sits at the edge of a vast swamp. Three weeks ago, people started disappearing. The village elder believes something ancient has woken in the ruins of the old watchtower deep in the marsh. She's right — but it's worse than she knows.

**The villain:** A Swamp Witch named Morra has taken up residence in the ruined watchtower. She is raising the dead from the old battleground nearby, building herself an undead army. She doesn't want to destroy Mirewatch — she wants to *own* it.

**The hook:** The heroes arrive in Mirewatch (as travellers, locals, or hired hands) and are approached by Elder Voss, a sharp old woman who offers them **15 gold** to find out what's happening in the swamp and put a stop to it.

---

## Chapter 1 — Something in the Swamp

*The heroes are level 1. This chapter introduces combat, exploration, and talking to NPCs.*

### Scene 1 — Mirewatch Village

Elder Voss explains the situation over a warm meal. Three villagers have vanished. Strange lights have been seen in the swamp at night. A hunter named Bod came back from the marsh babbling about "dead things walking" before collapsing from exhaustion.

**Roleplaying opportunities:**
- Talk to Bod (CHA check difficulty easy to calm him down enough to get useful information — he saw Skeleton Archers near the old road)
- Visit the market — heroes start with 10g each and can spend some here. A travelling merchant has Health Potions (3g), a Smoke Bomb (2g), and a battered Shortbow (3g)
- Ask around town — MND check difficulty 8 to recall that the old watchtower was abandoned after a battle between two warlords generations ago. Many soldiers were buried in the marsh.

### Scene 2 — The Marsh Road

The heroes travel into the swamp. The path is muddy, the air smells of rot, and the trees grow close. About an hour in, they're ambushed.

**Encounter: 3 Goblin Scouts**
*(Use the Goblin Scout stat block from the Bestiary)*

The goblins aren't working for Morra — they just saw some easy targets. After the fight, the heroes find a crude map on one of the goblins showing the watchtower's location, crudely drawn but useful.

> **GM tip:** Let the heroes try creative things here. If someone wants to intimidate the goblins into running (CHA check difficulty easy or medium), let them try. If they are struggling to beat them, make it so after 1 dies, the rest run away.

**Loot:** 4 gold, one Health Potion

### Scene 3 — The Buried Road

Further in, the heroes discover an old stone road half-sunken into the mud. Along it are freshly disturbed graves — mounds of earth broken open from *within*.

No combat here. This is atmosphere. A MND check (difficulty easy or medium) tells a hero that whatever raised these dead is powerful and intentional — this isn't random.

> **Level Up:** After returning to town and reporting what they've found, award the heroes **Level 2**. Elder Voss is frightened and doubles the reward to **30 gold** if they stop whatever is causing this.

---

## Chapter 2 — Into the Ruins

*The heroes are level 2. Combat gets more tactical. The threat becomes real.*

### Scene 4 — The Watchtower Approach

The ruined watchtower rises from the mist ahead. Its walls are crumbled but the base is intact. Torches burn in the windows. Before the heroes can get close, they must pass through the courtyard — which is guarded.

**Encounter: 4 Skeleton Archers**
*(Use the Skeleton Archer stat block from the Bestiary)*

The skeletons are positioned on rubble, shooting at range. Heroes who close the distance lose the archers' range advantage. A DEX check (difficulty medium) lets a hero sneak up before combat starts and get the drop on one skeleton.

**Loot:** Nothing — skeletons don't carry gold. But one has a strange symbol carved into its ribcage: Morra's mark. A MND check (difficulty medium) identifies it as a witch's binding rune.

### Scene 5 — The Tower Interior

Inside the tower, the heroes find signs of habitation — dried herbs, crow feathers, a cauldron, stolen supplies from Mirewatch. The three missing villagers are locked in a side room, alive and unharmed. Morra kept them as "guests" — she planned to use them as leverage.

**Roleplaying opportunity:** The villagers are scared but cooperative. One of them, a young woman named Syla, overheard Morra talking to her crows. Morra is planning to march on Mirewatch in two days. She's been raising the dead from the old battleground half a mile north — there could be dozens of them by now.

> **GM tip:** Let the heroes decide whether to fight Morra here, rescue the villagers and flee, or try to set a trap. All three are valid and lead to different versions of the final chapter.

### Scene 6 — Morra's Chamber

At the top of the tower, they find Morra — or she finds them. She doesn't attack immediately. She's theatrical, curious, and genuinely believes she's doing the village a favour by "protecting" it under her rule.

**This scene can go three ways:**

1. **Talk it out** — A CHA check (difficulty hard) to convince Morra that ruling a village of terrified, hostile people isn't worth her time. On success, she agrees to leave — and even gives the heroes a piece of loot as a "gesture of goodwill." She's pragmatic, not evil.
2. **Fight** — Use the Swamp Witch stat block from the Bestiary. Hard but winnable at level 2 with good tactics. If heroes use fire on her crows (they're flammable), they can neutralise her Crow Swarm ability.
3. **Make a deal** — A creative party might offer her something. A place to study in peace, a promise to keep hunters out of the deep marsh, or information about something she wants. The GM should reward this kind of thinking.

**Loot (if defeated or as a gift):** Ring of Mending, 12 gold, a Scroll of Lightning Bolt

> **Level Up:** Award **Level 3** after resolving the watchtower — whether through combat, negotiation, or clever thinking.

## Chapter 3 — The Dead March

*The heroes are level 3. This chapter only happens if Morra was defeated or fled — her undead army is now leaderless but still marching.*

> **Skip this chapter** if Morra was talked down and willingly stopped the ritual. Instead, jump to the Epilogue.

### Scene 7 — Race to the Battleground

The heroes have until dawn before the undead army reaches Mirewatch. They can either intercept it at the old battleground or make a stand at the village gates.

**Option A — The Battleground**
A disorganised mob of 6 Skeleton Archers and 4 Goblin Scouts (goblins who scavenged the area and got caught up in things). Without Morra directing them, the undead move slowly. Heroes who arrive first can set an ambush — a MND check (difficulty medium) to identify a chokepoint, giving all heroes advantage (roll 2d20 use highest roll) on the first round.

**Option B — The Village Gates**
Elder Voss and several villagers have armed themselves. The heroes fight alongside 3 Villager Allies (HP 8, Defense 9, attack: d20+2 vs Defense, d4 damage). This is messier and more dramatic — and someone might get hurt.

> **GM tip:** Let the heroes make a plan before this encounter. Even a rough plan should get some mechanical reward — maybe advantage on initiative, or one free round before the undead arrive.

### Scene 8 — Epilogue

With the threat ended, Mirewatch celebrates. Elder Voss pays the heroes their 50 gold reward and offers them a permanent room at the inn "whenever you need it."

Syla the villager thanks them personally and mentions she's heard rumours of trouble further east — a collapsed mine, strange lights, missing travellers. A new adventure, if the heroes want it.

> **Level Up:** Award **Level 4** for completing the campaign.

## GM Notes

- **Tone:** This campaign works well as light adventure or a slightly spooky fairy tale. Morra is more interesting as a grey character than a pure villain — lean into that.
- **Pacing:** Each chapter is one session. If your group talks a lot, Chapter 2 alone could be a full session.
- **Scaling:** For only 1-2 heroes, reduce Goblin Scout encounters to 2 enemies and give the heroes a free Health Potion before the Morra fight. For 4+ heroes, add one extra Skeleton Archer per courtyard wave.
- **What comes next?** Syla's rumour about the eastern mine is an easy hook into a second short campaign — darker, underground, and featuring the Cave Troll from the Bestiary.
